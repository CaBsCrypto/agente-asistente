# Carmelita brand system

**Status:** working brand direction, not a completed trademark clearance  
**Version:** 0.1 — 2026-07-22  
**Applies to:** product UI, landing page, developer documentation, partner material and YC application

## 1. Brand decision

**Carmelita** is the working public name for the product and the company narrative.

It should feel like a capable personal agent, not a crypto wallet, chatbot or infrastructure protocol. The product can search, prepare and execute actions, but the user remains the source of authority.

### Category

**Personal execution agent**

Use this category before broader descriptions such as “AI assistant” or “agentic commerce infrastructure.” It explains the defining behavior: Carmelita moves from conversation to a real, auditable result.

### Primary positioning

> Carmelita is a personal AI agent with memory, permissions and a wallet that safely executes real-world actions.

### Primary tagline

> **Knows you. Acts for you.**

Translations:

- Spanish: **Te conoce. Actúa por ti.**
- Portuguese: **Conhece você. Age por você.**

### Safety line

> **Within your rules.**

Use this directly after the primary tagline, or beside approvals, limits and policy controls. It explains how Carmelita acts for the user without weakening the warmer primary promise.

### Supporting line

> Ask Carmelita to find it, prepare it and get it done.

Prefer “Ask Carmelita. She prepares it. You decide.” It communicates assistance and user control without implying that credentials, funds, partner access or approval are unnecessary.

## 2. Why the name works

Carmelita is warm, pronounceable and personal. It gives the assistant a recognizable identity while leaving room for bookings, payments, travel, knowledge, business integrations and future channels.

The visual story may be **inspired by carmeltazite**, a mineral whose name comes from Mount Carmel and its dominant titanium, aluminum and zirconium. Material containing it has been marketed as “Carmel Sapphire.” Carmelita itself is **not** the name of a gemstone, and brand copy must never claim that it is.

Reference material:

- [Mindat: Carmeltazite mineral data](https://www.mindat.org/min-53294.html)
- [International Gemological Institute: Carmel Sapphire](https://www.igi.org/trapiche-gemstones-and-carmel-sapphire/)

This origin story is optional. The product must make sense without explaining it.

Write the name exactly as **Carmelita**. Do not stylize it as CarmelITA, CARMELita or Carmel.AI.

| Language | Spoken guide | Guidance |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Spanish | `kar-meh-LEE-tah` | Natural Spanish stress on “li.” |
| English | `kar-meh-LEE-tah` | Four clear syllables; do not shorten to “Carmel.” |
| Portuguese | `kar-meh-LEE-tah` | Keep the hard initial C and stress “li.” |

For an English launch video, introduce it once as: “Carmelita — car-meh-LEE-tah.” Do not repeatedly explain the pronunciation.

## 4. Brand promise and proof

### Promise

Carmelita turns a user’s intent into a controlled, observable action across digital and real-world services.

### Product pillars

1. **Knows** — remembers explicit preferences, context and policies.
2. **Connects** — reaches services through APIs, MCP and approved integrations.
3. **Prepares** — discovers options and builds a reviewable intent before acting.
4. **Protects** — applies limits, approvals, replay protection and non-custodial boundaries.
5. **Acts** — completes a booking, payment or onchain operation and returns evidence.

### Proof order

Always present evidence in this order:

1. What a new user can do now.
2. What has been executed on Testnet or against a live partner API.
3. The receipt, transaction hash or partner response.
4. The safety boundary demonstrated.
5. What remains a preview, simulation or partner dependency.

## 5. Messaging hierarchy by audience

### End users

**Headline:** Tell Carmelita what you need. Review the important decisions. Let it handle the steps.

Emphasize:

- one conversation instead of navigating many applications;
- personal memory and explicit rules;
- the user’s own wallet and credentials;
- clear approval moments and receipts;
- English, Spanish and Portuguese access.

Avoid leading with MCP, x402, Soroban, Privy or orchestration graphs.

### Developers

**Headline:** Give your service a safe path from agent intent to verified execution.

Emphasize:

- inbound and outbound MCP/API connectivity;
- stable tool contracts and structured intents;
- identity and per-user authorization context;
- prepare → policy → approve → execute-once → evidence;
- sandbox/Testnet support and explicit production boundaries.

Use the product name in examples, but keep route names, JSON fields, tool names and error codes exact.

### Merchants and service providers

**Headline:** Make your service discoverable and actionable by personal AI agents.

Emphasize:

- progressive onboarding: listing, connector, API/MCP or enterprise integration;
- bookings and orders before complex settlement;
- the merchant remains the system of record;
- no forced replacement of an existing checkout or operational system;
- verifiable requests, customer consent and receipts.

Do not promise automatic access to a company’s private product or account without its API, authorization mechanism or approved browser integration.

### YC and investors

**Headline:** Carmelita is the execution layer between personal AI and the services people already use.

Lead with:

1. the demoable user loop;
2. the wedge in Latin America;
3. validated transactions and partner actions;
4. the non-custodial authorization architecture;
5. the expansion path from one agent to a service network.

Recommended short description:

> Carmelita lets people complete bookings and payments by talking to an AI agent. It remembers their preferences, applies their rules and uses their own wallet or connected account. We have demonstrated live service actions and replay-resistant Stellar Testnet transactions without taking custody of user funds.

Never describe an integration, volume, customer, revenue or production payment as live unless current evidence supports it.

## 6. Voice and writing

### Personality

- **Warm, not cute.** Carmelita is approachable without becoming a mascot for children.
- **Capable, not magical.** Explain what will happen and what is required.
- **Calm, not passive.** Recommend a next action without pressuring the user.
- **Precise, not technical by default.** Reveal implementation details when relevant.
- **Transparent, not alarming.** Surface risk, cost, network and approval state before execution.

### Conversation pattern

1. Confirm the outcome in plain language.
2. State any missing connection, balance or choice.
3. Show the proposed action and material consequences.
4. Ask for approval only when policy requires it.
5. Execute once.
6. Return evidence and a useful next step.

Preferred:

> I found three available dates. Friday costs one credit. I can prepare the reservation now; you will review it before it is confirmed.

Avoid:

> Your request has been autonomously completed across our revolutionary agentic infrastructure.

### Vocabulary

Prefer: **ask, connect, prepare, review, approve, execute, receipt, your wallet, your rules, Testnet**.

Use carefully: **autonomous, automatic, safe, verified, guaranteed, escrow, yield, production**.

Avoid in user-facing headlines: **gateway, protocol, MCP server, x402 facilitator, orchestration runtime**.

## 7. Product naming architecture

Use a single master brand. Product names describe capabilities; they are not independent brands.

| Name | Purpose | User-facing label |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Carmelita Agent** | Conversational workspace and action orchestration | Carmelita |
| **Carmelita Memory** | Personal knowledge, preferences and rules | Memory |
| **Carmelita Wallet** | User-owned wallet, balances and signing surface | Wallet |
| **Carmelita Connect** | APIs, MCP, OAuth and partner connectors | Connections |
| **Carmelita Actions** | Action catalog, intents, approvals and receipts | Actions |
| **Carmelita Business** | Merchant onboarding and provider administration | For businesses |

Rules:

- Say **Carmelita** when referring to the assistant or overall experience.
- Use the full product name in documentation titles and partner materials.
- Use the shorter user-facing label inside navigation where space is limited.
- Do not create separate logos or color palettes for each capability.
- Do not call the user’s memory “Carmelita’s memory”; it is the **user’s Memory in Carmelita**.
- Keep the wallet explicitly user-owned; avoid phrases such as “Carmelita’s funds.”

These names are brand vocabulary, not claims about mineral composition or gemstone color.

### Risk colors

Color is supplemental; every state also needs text and an icon.

| Level | Color | Required language |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Informational | `#445B8F` | “No funds or external state will change.” |
| Low | `#2F7A58` | “Reversible or read-only.” |
| Medium | `#B57713` | “External state will change.” |
| High | `#C63F2A` | “Funds, credentials or irreversible action involved.” |

Never use green alone to mean “approved” or red alone to mean “blocked.”

### Typography

- Use **Inter** or the existing system sans stack for interface and body text.
- Use the existing monospace stack only for statuses, IDs, network names and receipts.
- Headlines should be direct, sentence case and tightly spaced.
- Do not use a decorative gemstone font or script typeface.

### Shape and imagery

- Rounded surfaces communicate approachability.
- Faceted details may appear in the mark, dividers or diagrams, never as noisy card decoration.
- Use real product evidence—receipts, action previews and connection states—before abstract AI imagery.
- Avoid robots, brains, glowing coins, crystal balls and generic humanoid assistants.

## 9. Logo direction

The mark combines:

- a faceted **C**, representing Carmelita, conversation and connection;
- an open right side, representing movement from intent toward execution;
- a detached coral diamond, representing the explicit permission boundary before action.

Reference assets:

- `/public/brand/carmelita-mark.svg`
- `/public/brand/carmelita-lockup.svg`
- `/public/brand/carmelita-mark-mono.svg`
- `/public/brand/carmelita-lockup-reverse.svg`

Logo rules:

- Keep clear space equal to the width of the permission diamond.
- Minimum mark size: 24 CSS pixels.
- Use the lockup on light or cream backgrounds.
- Use the reverse lockup on Carmel Forest or similarly dark backgrounds.
- Use the monochrome mark only when the two-color asset is not technically possible.
- At small sizes use the mark, not the letter C alone.
- Do not close the gap, attach the permission diamond, rotate the mark, add gradients, or recolor individual elements.
- Treat these files as a v0 direction, not a legally cleared final trademark.

## 10. Technical identity and URL policy

The public brand can change without immediately renaming technical contracts.

### Preserve during the first migration

- Production origin: `https://agente-asistente.vercel.app`
- Repository: `CaBsCrypto/agente-asistente`
- npm/package identifier: `agente-asistente`
- Existing `/api/*`, `/api/mcp*` and `/.well-known/mcp*` routes
- Database table and column names
- Environment variable names
- MCP tool names, JSON fields, error codes and idempotency keys
- Browser extension host permissions
- Stellar registration records and public agent metadata until a coordinated update

These are compatibility identifiers, not the public-facing brand. Existing clients must continue to work.

### Safe display-name changes

After approval, UI copy and metadata may show:

- `Carmelita`
- `Carmelita Agent`
- `Carmelita Wallet — Testnet`
- `Carmelita MCP Gateway`

Health responses and MCP `serverInfo.name` should not change until clients, monitoring and documentation have been audited. When changed, consider preserving a stable machine `id` and changing only a human-readable `title` where the protocol supports it.

### Future domain transition

Do not select or announce a new domain until ownership and trademark screening are complete. When a domain is acquired:

1. Add it as an alias before making it canonical.
2. Keep the Vercel origin operational.
3. Update OAuth/Privy allowed origins and redirect URIs.
4. Update CORS, CSP, webhook allowlists and browser extension permissions.
5. Update MCP discovery and Stellar registration metadata.
6. Publish redirects only after callbacks and signed webhooks have been tested.
7. Retain old API URLs for at least one documented compatibility window.

## 12. Prohibited or gated claims

Do not publish these claims without the indicated proof:

| Claim | Why prohibited or gated |
| --- | --- |
| “Carmelita is a gemstone.” | False. The brand is only inspired by carmeltazite/Carmel Sapphire. |
| “The first personal AI agent” | Requires defensible market research and is unlikely to remain true. |
| “Works with every app/business.” | Access depends on a connector, API, MCP, OAuth or approved interaction surface. |
| “Fully autonomous” | The product applies rules and may require approval, credentials or funds. |
| “100% safe” or “risk-free” | No software, payment or investment action can support that absolute claim. |
| “Non-custodial” | Use only for flows where the user controls the wallet and Carmelita cannot unilaterally transfer funds. |
| “Production payments” | Current Testnet evidence does not prove production settlement. |
| “Guaranteed yield/returns” | Financial outcomes cannot be guaranteed. |
| “Verified merchant/agent” | Requires a defined verification process and current evidence. |
| “Escrow” | Requires an implemented contract, legal review and documented release/dispute rules. |
| “Connected to [partner]” | Use only when the current integration and authorization path have been validated. |
| “AI remembers everything” | Memory is scoped, fallible and controlled by storage/retention settings. |

## 13. Brand-risk note

The initial name search is **not legal trademark clearance**. “Carmelita” has existing cultural, geographic, musical, character and personal-name uses. Search results without an obvious direct AI-agent collision do not establish availability.

Before investing in a domain, company registration, app-store launch or paid campaign:

1. Search exact and similar marks in the relevant trademark databases.
2. Review software, financial services, travel, commerce and telecommunications classes with counsel.
3. Check phonetic and visual similarity, not only exact spelling.
4. Verify domains, social handles, package registries and app stores.
5. Document the countries and services covered by the clearance.

Until that work is complete, label Carmelita internally as a **working brand**.

## 14. Definition of done

The transition is ready to ship when:

- one positioning sentence is consistent across landing, README, YC material and demo;
- the UI distinguishes read-only, prepared, approved and executed states;
- every live claim has a reproducible receipt or acceptance test;
- legacy URLs and machine identifiers remain compatible;
- EN/ES/PT copy uses the same promises and safety boundaries;
- legal/name screening has an owner and written outcome;
- a new-user acceptance test passes without relying on the founder’s account.
